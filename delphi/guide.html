Getting to Delphi

By Car

Delphi is accessible by road from Athens, approximately 180 km northwest of the capital.

  • Travel time: around 2.5 hours
  • Scenic drive through mountains and countryside
  • Car rental options available at Athens Airport

By Bus

Regular buses run from Athens to Delphi, departing from KTEL Liosion Bus Station.

  • Travel time: around 3 hours
  • Direct routes available
  • Cost: approximately €15 one-way

Where to Stay

Recommended Areas

  • Delphi Town - Close to the archaeological site and museum
  • Arachova - A nearby mountain village with traditional charm
  • Itea - Coastal town for those preferring a seaside stay

Accommodation Types

Type Price Range (€/night)
Guesthouses 30-60
Mid-range Hotels 60-100
Luxury Boutique Hotels 120+

Getting Around

Walking

Most sites in Delphi can be explored on foot, as the town is compact and pedestrian-friendly.

  • Archaeological site and museum within walking distance
  • Hiking trails with scenic views available nearby

Local Buses & Taxis

Buses and taxis are available for trips to nearby towns like Arachova and Itea.

  • Bus to Arachova: frequent service
  • Taxi fares are affordable for short distances

Practical Information

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (Apr-May): Mild temperatures, vibrant scenery
  • Fall (Sep-Oct): Pleasant weather and fewer crowds
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): Warm, but popular for tourists
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): Quieter with potential snow in Arachova

Money Tips

  • ATMs available in Delphi and Arachova
  • Credit cards widely accepted
  • Carry small cash for local shops and cafes
  • Tipping: 5-10% is appreciated

Safety & Emergency

Safety Tips

  • Respect the archaeological sites; follow all posted signs
  • Wear sturdy shoes for uneven terrain
  • Stay hydrated, especially in warmer months
  • In winter, be cautious of icy conditions in mountainous areas

Emergency Numbers

  • Emergency: 112
  • Police: 100
  • Ambulance: 166
